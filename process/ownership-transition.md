# Ownership Transition Workflow

## Purpose

Assess ownership transfer, succession, and leadership-change situations that could alter franchise stability, approval risk, or brokerage continuity.

Use this workflow when the evidence suggests a sale, succession event, APA/IBA dependency, incoming operator, or meaningful leadership turnover.

## When To Use

Use this workflow when any of these appear in the user request or source evidence:

- ownership change, succession, retirement, estate event, partner exit, or sale process
- APA, IBA, transfer approval, legal closing, financing, or onboarding dependency
- incoming operator or buyer with unclear readiness or alignment
- manager, broker-owner, or operating leader turnover tied to brokerage health
- questions about continuity, retention during transition, or territorial implications

## Required Evidence Checklist

Gather or mark unknown:

- current owner or controlling party
- incoming owner, buyer, successor, or interim leader if one exists
- stage: `confirmed`, `in progress`, `rumored`, or `stale/unclear`
- transaction or approval dependencies such as APA, IBA, legal review, financing, or franchisor approval
- continuity indicators: brokerage leadership, key team retention, operating capability, support dependency
- affected offices, territories, agent populations, or brands if available
- open compliance, legal, or commercial blockers

Do not invent ownership stage, approval status, or succession readiness.

## Evaluation Rules

- Separate `ownership change is happening` from `transition is stable`. Closing risk and operating risk are not the same.
- Treat unresolved APA/IBA, approval, legal, or financing items as material because they can stall or destabilize the relationship.
- Make leadership continuity explicit. A technically progressing sale may still create retention or service instability if operator continuity is weak.
- Classify whether the main exposure is continuity, approval, retention, territory, compliance, or competitive vulnerability.
- If the transition appears completed, still test whether the source evidence shows a post-close stabilization gap.

## Questions To Answer In Layered Output

- What is changing in control, leadership, or succession?
- What dependencies could delay, weaken, or destabilize the transfer?
- How could the transition affect agent retention, owner confidence, service delivery, or territory position?
- What should be clarified before escalating the situation as healthy or at risk?

## Layer Guidance

- `summary.md` `## Memory`: preserve durable facts about ownership structure, succession pattern, and stable dependencies.
- `summary.md` `## Tensions`: capture unresolved approval, continuity, or retention pressure.
- `summary.md` `## Insight`: explain what the transition means now for franchise stability.
- `summary.md` `## Proposed Actions`: recommend a narrow set of clarification, support, or escalation options tied to the transition stage.

## Do Not Conclude Transition Stability When

- a transaction is mentioned once but no stage is confirmed
- legal documents are referenced without evidence of completion or blockers
- leadership continuity is assumed from title changes alone
- the analysis ignores agent or office-level disruption during the transfer
