# Renewal Workflow

## Purpose

Assess renewal readiness, churn risk, and commercial pressure for a franchise relationship.

Use this workflow when the evidence points to contract timing, renewal posture, owner confidence, unresolved service value, or brand-switch risk around term decisions.

## When To Use

Use this workflow when any of these appear in the user request or source evidence:

- contract end date, renewal date, anniversary, term discussion, or renewal planning
- owner sentiment about staying, leaving, or renegotiating
- unresolved service, legal, finance, support, or brand-value issues tied to staying in the system
- competitor mentions near a renewal decision window
- brokerage decline, agent-count loss, or leadership instability that could change renewal posture

## Required Evidence Checklist

Gather or mark unknown:

- account and relevant owner or decision-maker
- contract end date, renewal timing, or best available term signal
- current brokerage health evidence if available: agent movement, growth/decline, leadership stability, office footprint
- service, support, legal, finance, or commercial issues that could affect renewal
- competitor or alternative-brand references tied to the account
- whether the relationship appears `confirmed`, `in progress`, `rumored`, or `stale/unclear`

Do not infer missing dates, decision-makers, or renewal stage.

## Evaluation Rules

- Separate `renewal timing risk` from `renewal relationship risk`. An account can be far from term but still show early churn pressure.
- Distinguish dissatisfaction from departure intent. Complaints alone are not churn unless the evidence connects them to renewal posture.
- Treat unresolved commercial, legal, or service issues as material when they sit near a renewal window or owner-confidence discussion.
- Name whether the renewal signal is owner-led, operations-led, competitor-led, or performance-led.
- If the evidence is thin, state what is unknown rather than implying a renewal forecast.

## Questions To Answer In Layered Output

- What is the current renewal stage and how confident are we?
- What business mechanism could push this relationship toward renewal stability or churn?
- Is the issue about value delivery, economics, leadership confidence, competitive alternatives, or brokerage performance?
- What must be clarified before anyone escalates or proposes retention action?

## Layer Guidance

- `summary.md` `## Memory`: preserve only durable facts that will matter beyond the immediate renewal cycle.
- `summary.md` `## Tensions`: capture current renewal pressure, unresolved blockers, and timing sensitivity.
- `summary.md` `## Insight`: state what the current evidence means for retention and owner confidence now.
- `summary.md` `## Proposed Actions`: recommend a small number of renewal-focused next moves without inventing owners, dates, or external tasks.

## Do Not Conclude Renewal Risk When

- the only evidence is a stale contract field with no current relationship signal
- service complaints exist but no business consequence is visible
- a competitor is mentioned once without any tie to the account's decision-making
- the renewal stage depends on assumed dates or inferred owner intent
