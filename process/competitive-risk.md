# Competitive Risk Workflow

## Purpose

Assess competitor pressure against the franchise relationship, including poaching, conversion risk, market encroachment, and service-value vulnerability.

Use this workflow when the evidence suggests an external brand or brokerage could weaken affiliation, retention, recruiting, or owner confidence.

## When To Use

Use this workflow when any of these appear in the user request or source evidence:

- competitor brand or brokerage mentioned in notes, contact fields, or account discussions
- future affiliation or switch signal involving another brand
- recruiting losses, poaching concerns, or owner comparison against alternatives
- market-share pressure, territorial encroachment, or service comparisons that increase switch risk
- repeated references to the same external competitor across related contacts or accounts

## Required Evidence Checklist

Gather or mark unknown:

- competitor identity
- asset at risk: agent, team, owner, office, account, territory, or renewal
- threat mechanism: poach, switch, commercial comparison, succession option, service dissatisfaction, or market encroachment
- freshness and stage: `confirmed`, `in progress`, `rumored`, or `stale/unclear`
- scope: `isolated`, `team`, `brokerage`, or `market`
- evidence of whether the signal is repeated or singular
- current business consequence if the threat materializes

Do not assume competitor intent, scale, or timing without evidence.

## Evaluation Rules

- Separate `competitor mention` from `competitive risk`. A named competitor matters only when the evidence shows a mechanism that could change affiliation or confidence.
- Name the threatened asset precisely. Risk to one agent is not the same as risk to a renewal, office, or territory.
- Distinguish isolated poaching from patterned competitive pressure across the same brokerage or market.
- Treat competitor signals as stronger when paired with internal dissatisfaction, ownership instability, weak service value, or recent attrition.
- If the evidence is thin, keep the conclusion at monitoring or clarification level.

## Questions To Answer In Layered Output

- Who is the competitor and what exactly is at risk?
- Is the threat isolated, repeated, or structurally tied to a broader weakness?
- What internal weakness, if any, makes the relationship vulnerable?
- What clarification or intervention is needed before escalation?

## Layer Guidance

- `summary.md` `## Memory`: preserve durable competitor context only when the pattern is stable enough to matter later.
- `summary.md` `## Tensions`: capture current external pressure and the internal weakness that makes it relevant.
- `summary.md` `## Insight`: explain what the competitor signal means now for retention, growth, or territory position.
- `summary.md` `## Proposed Actions`: recommend targeted monitoring, clarification, retention, or escalation options without inventing external tasks.

## Do Not Conclude Competitive Threat When

- the competitor appears only as a vague reference with no affected asset
- no business mechanism links the competitor to affiliation, renewal, or recruiting outcome
- one stale affiliation field is treated as market-level competitive pressure
- the analysis ignores whether the risk is really internal service dissatisfaction rather than external competitive pull
